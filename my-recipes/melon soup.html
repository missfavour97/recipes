Melon Soup

Description

the Nigerian Melon Soup is a rich, flavorful dish made with ground melon seeds, vegetables, palm oil, and assorted meats or fish.

Ingredients

  • Ground melon seeds (egusi)
  • Palm oil
  • Assorted meats (beef, goat meat, tripe, etc.)
  • Stock fish or dried fish
  • Crayfish (ground)
  • Onions
  • Fresh or dried pepper (Scotch bonnet or chili)
  • Seasoning cubes (Maggi or Knorr)
  • Salt
  • Bitter leaf or spinach (ugu, pumpkin leaves, or kale as substitute)
  • Water or meat stock
  • Ground pepper or paprika (optional for extra spice)

Steps;

  1. Blend melon seeds (egusi) and set aside.
  2. Wash and season your meats with salt, onion, and seasoning cubes, then boil until tender.
  3. Once cooked, set the meat aside but keep the stock (broth).
  4. Heat palm oil in a pot, add chopped onions, then add the ground egusi.
  5. Stir and fry gently until it thickens and forms small lumps.
  6. Pour in the meat stock gradually, add the cooked meats, crayfish, and pepper.
  7. Stir and let it cook for about 10 to 15 minutes.
  8. Stir in the washed bitter leaf or spinach.
  9. Cook for another 5 minutes until everything blends well.
  10. Adjust seasoning with salt or cubes if needed.
  11. Serve hot with pounded yam, eba, or fufu.
